Documentation - QuickStart

1. Customer Journey:

  • The customer journey is the complete sum of experiences that customers go through when interacting with your company and brand. A journey consists of a sequential sequence of touchpoints. A touchpoint is defined as an interaction between the customer and the company through a particular channel, in a particular period. To describe the content of a touchpoint, we have an action.
  • For example, a customer made a transaction to buy a ball from 3:00 pm to 3:02 pm through an official website of the company. In this example, the process when the customer interacts with the company (from 3:00 pm to 3:02 pm) is considered a touchpoint, while the website is supposed to be the channel where the touchpoint took place. In this example, the action of this touchpoint is “buy” or “make a transaction”

2. Customer Journey Master:

Journey Master is a customer journey management system implemented based on the process mining framework. Using the information extracted from the company’s event log, the Journey Master system provides 2 main central features:

  • Mapping, modeling, and visualize the journey
  • Clustering the journeys based on their similarities, which helps statist view the main flow of the customer’s intention.
  • Determine decision factors influencing customer journey
  • Making daily reports: action, device, geo-network, channel, ...
